# Citybuilder Tech Tree

## Core Structure

The construction progression is built around one main branch:

**Scrap -> Earth -> Timber -> Masonry -> Brick -> Industrial Block -> Reinforced Concrete -> Steel Frame -> Prestige**

Each material tier depends on 3 support branches:

- **Tools**
- **Machines**
- **Workforce / Knowledge**

So the player does not just unlock "brick" magically.
They need:
- the right raw resources
- the right tools or production building
- the right trained workers
- the right research

In addition, many tools, stations, vehicles, and machines also depend on:

- **Fuel**
- **Consumable production inputs**
- **Replacement parts**
- **Electricity / power distribution**
- **Upstream machine industries**

That means later construction is not only about materials, but also about maintaining an industrial ecosystem that can keep tools, vehicles, and production systems running.

# 5. Machine Dependency Layer

Machines should not exist as isolated unlocks.
Most of them should depend on an upstream chain of materials, parts, fuel, maintenance, and sometimes electricity.

This creates a believable industrial progression and also prevents late-game construction from feeling magical.

## Core Machine Dependency Types

### Structural Inputs
The machine itself must be built out of something.

Examples:
- Steel frame
- Iron gears
- Cast housings
- Copper wiring
- Rubber belts
- Glass gauges
- Control panels

### Consumable Inputs
The machine consumes these while operating.

Examples:
- Fuel
- Electricity
- Lubricant
- Coolant
- Welding gas
- Cutting fluid
- Abrasive discs

### Maintenance Inputs
The machine slowly wears down and needs support goods.

Examples:
- Spare parts
- Bearings
- Replacement belts
- Hydraulic fluid
- Repair kits
- New blades
- Tire replacement

### Workforce Inputs
A machine may exist, but not function without the right staff.

Examples:
- Mechanic
- Machine operator
- Electrician
- Heavy equipment operator
- Engineer

### Infrastructure Inputs
Advanced machines may need support networks.

Examples:
- Road access
- Fuel depot
- Power grid
- Water supply
- Industrial storage
- Repair workshop

# 8. Machine-to-Machine Production Examples

A strong late-game progression should include the idea that machines help build other machines.

This is one of the best ways to make industrial growth feel deep and earned.

## Example Chain A — From Forge to Crane

1. **Forge** makes tool heads, brackets, bolts, and simple fittings  
2. **Machine Shop** uses those parts to make shafts, gears, housings, and motor mounts  
3. **Steel Mill** provides beams, plates, and structural sections  
4. **Cable Workshop** produces lifting cable  
5. **Electrical Workshop** makes motor housings, switchgear, and control boxes  
6. **Heavy Factory** assembles the final crane  

**Result:** the player needs several industries cooperating before advanced lifting systems exist.

---

## Example Chain B — From Timber Industry to Concrete Plant

1. **Sawmill** makes formwork planks and packaging crates  
2. **Forge** makes metal fasteners and brackets  
3. **Machine Shop** makes rotating mixer components  
4. **Foundry** casts heavy housings and drum rings  
5. **Electrical Shop** makes starter systems or electric motors  
6. **Assembly Workshop** produces the mixer or batching unit  
7. **Concrete Plant** becomes available  

**Result:** timber, metal, casting, and machine production all matter.

---

## Example Chain C — From Basic Smithing to Excavator

1. **Forge** makes simple steel parts  
2. **Foundry** produces engine block castings  
3. **Machine Shop** machines shafts, pistons, couplings, and gearboxes  
4. **Rubber Plant** makes hoses, seals, and tracks or tires  
5. **Electrical Workshop** makes gauges, ignition systems, and wiring harnesses  
6. **Hydraulic Workshop** makes cylinders and pumps  
7. **Heavy Vehicle Factory** assembles the excavator  

**Result:** a heavy vehicle becomes a major industrial milestone instead of a simple unlock.

---

## Example Chain D — Precision Industry for Luxury Buildings

1. **Quarry** extracts marble or granite  
2. **Stone Cutting Shop** slices slabs  
3. **Polishing Workshop** finishes surfaces  
4. **Glassworks** creates luxury glazing  
5. **Metal Fabrication Shop** creates decorative bronze, railings, and fittings  
6. **Electrical Workshop** makes climate-control and lighting systems  
7. **Prestige Construction Yard** assembles premium building kits  

**Result:** luxury buildings feel like the output of a rich advanced city.

# 15. Simple Rules for Gameplay

## Rule 1 — Hand tools need materials but usually not power
Examples:
- Hammer
- Saw
- Trowel
- Chisel

Common needs:
- Metal
- Wood handles
- Repairs

---

## Rule 2 — Stations need fuel or labor throughput
Examples:
- Forge
- Kiln
- Sawmill
- Stone cutter

Common needs:
- Fuel
- Input materials
- Repair materials
- Workers

---

## Rule 3 — Machines need maintenance plus fuel or electricity
Examples:
- Mixer
- Press
- Crane
- Excavator

Common needs:
- Spare parts
- Lubricants
- Trained operators
- Grid or fuel depot access

---

## Rule 4 — Advanced machines should require components made by other industries
Examples:
- Motors
- Bearings
- Hydraulics
- Electronics
- Precision castings

This prevents late-game progression from feeling too flat.

---

## Rule 5 — Building shells and building operation can be separate
A player may be able to construct:
- a hospital shell
- a tower shell
- a museum shell

But to activate full function, the building may also need:
- interior furniture
- service objects
- electricity
- heat
- maintenance workers
- operating supplies

This creates deeper city management.

# 16. Unlock Logic

Each construction tier should require 4 conditions:

## Resource Gate
Do you have the raw inputs?

## Tool Gate
Do workers have the proper tools?

## Production Gate
Do you have the station or machine needed?

## Workforce Gate
Do you have trained people to do the work?

That means progression feels earned instead of arbitrary.

For advanced tools, machines, vehicles, and high-tier buildings, add 3 more gates:

## Fuel / Power Gate
Can you actually run the equipment or building?

## Maintenance Gate
Can you replace worn parts and keep systems working?

## Service / Interior Gate
Can the finished building be furnished and operated properly?

# 18. Best Simple Version for the Actual Game

If you want the cleanest playable structure, use this:

## Main Material Progression
**Scrap -> Earth -> Timber -> Stone/Brick -> Block/Concrete -> Reinforced Concrete -> Steel/Glass -> Prestige**

## Tool Progression
**Scavenged -> Crude -> Trade -> Professional -> Industrial -> Heavy**

## Workforce Progression
**Labor -> Primitive Builders -> Apprentices -> Skilled Trades -> Industrial Trades -> Engineers -> Masters**

## Machine Progression
**None -> Workbench -> Sawmill/Forge/Kiln -> Quarry/Brickworks -> Cement/Block Industry -> Crane/Concrete Systems -> Steel/Luxury Industry**

## Energy Progression
**Human/Animal -> Biomass Fuel -> Coal/Coke -> Oil Fuels -> Electricity -> Advanced Hybrid Power**

## Interior / Service Progression
**Bare Shelter -> Heated Basic Interior -> Serviced Civic Interior -> Electrified Standard Interior -> Advanced Comfort Interior**

# 19. Why This Version Works Better

This structure is better because:
- it has one obvious main branch
- support systems are separated cleanly
- each tier has a clear identity
- each unlock tells the player what it actually gives them
- machines feel physically grounded in fuel, power, maintenance, and upstream industry
- buildings can differ both by shell quality and by interior / service level
- it feels like a real game progression instead of a reference chart
